Contact

If you have any questions or suggestions, please feel free to contact the team below. For feature and pull requests, please use the github repositories listed in the Downloads section.

Kerstin Bach

Project lead for myCBR. Kerstin's research interests include AI-driven decision support, case-based reasoning, intelligent sensing, and knowledge management, with a strong emphasis on healthcare applications. Her recent works focus on the explainability and interpretability of machine learning methods. She has been working on myCBR since 2010. For more information, please visit Kerstin's webpage

Pascal Reuss

Project co-lead for myCBR. Pascal's research interests are the development of intelligent information systems covering industrial, and societal applications. His work covers hybrid CBR systems as well as intelligent agents in games.

Former Team Members

Over the years many people supported the development of myCBR in different roles:

  • Armin Stahl was together with Thomas Roth-Berghofer the initiator of the myCBR tool and co-lead the development of the tool at DFKI.
  • Klaus-Dieter Althoff has been the head of the Competence Center for CBR at DFKI where myCBR has been further developed.
  • Daniel Bahls developed the initial version of the myCBR Protégé plugin and some of its explanation capabilities.
  • Michael Loescher and Andreas Rumpf contributed and helped a great deal in debugging the myCBR Protégé plugin.
  • Laura Zilles took over from Daniel Bahls. She designed and developed the myCBR SDK, the core of the current OSGi version of the myCBR Workbench.
  • Fabian Schwahn ported myCBR to the OSGi platform.
